Workshop on Liquid Antennas for Reconfigurable and Intelligent Wireless Systems

Abstract

The rapid evolution of wireless communications and the advent of 6G demands a paradigm shift in antenna design. Static, rigid hardware is giving way to dynamic, adaptable, and intelligent systems capable of meeting the demands of ultra-high reliability, low latency, and massive connectivity. This workshop, “Liquid Antennas for Reconfigurable and Intelligent Wireless Systems,” is positioned at the forefront of this transformation. It provides a holistic reference for the latest research, focusing on liquid antennas as a key enabler within the broader framework of next-generation reconfigurable wireless technologies.

The focus is to explore the entire value chain from soft-matter physics to AI-driven control algorithms. At the material level, we will delve into the science, fabrication, and modeling of liquid antennas, whose inherent flexibility and stretchability make them ideal for next-generation wireless communication devices. Building on this physical foundation, we expand our scope to the broader paradigm of the Fluid Antenna System (FAS), where these and other software-controllable structures—such as RF pixels and metasurfaces—reconfigure shape, position, and radiation characteristics. Unlike traditional antennas, the fine spatial resolution of FAS unlocks unprecedented performance gains. The aim is to foster a vibrant dialogue among material scientists, antenna engineers, and communication theorists, catalyzing collaborations that will define the future of wireless front-ends.

The topics of interest include but are not limited to

  • Novel liquid antenna materials, designs, and fabrication techniques
  • Architectures, feeding networks, and beamforming strategies for liquid-based antenna arrays
  • Design of filters, phase shifters, and other RF components using liquid materials
  • Novel designs and fabrication techniques for reconfigurable antennas and Intelligent wireless systems
  • Electromagnetic and multiphysics modeling of fluid antenna system (FAS)s and their dynamic reconfiguration
  • Information-theoretic analysis and advanced optimization algorithms for FAS performance
  • Machine learning for channel estimation, resource allocation, and intelligent control protocols
  • Multiple access schemes, joint communication and sensing (JCAS), and security enhancements for advanced communication
  • Hardware designs and signal processing for integrated sensing using fluid antennas
  • Prototyping, experimental validation, and integration into real-world applications
