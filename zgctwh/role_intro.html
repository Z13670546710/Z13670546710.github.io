Festivals And Customs

the Spring Festival

The first day of the first lunar month is the most important traditional festival for Chinese people. On New Year's Eve, the whole family gathers to watch the New Year's Eve and share a sumptuous New Year's Eve dinner. Dumplings are a must eat in the north to symbolize "getting married in the new year", while rice cakes are commonly used in the south to symbolize "rising every year".

Loong Boat Festival

The fifth day of the fifth lunar month was originally a festival for disease prevention and control, later combined with the legend of commemorating Qu Yuan. Every family packs Zongzi, and uses Ruo leaves to wrap glutinous rice. The stuffing varies from place to place, ranging from sweet to salty. Loong Boat races were held in various places, and the drums were beating to the sky to reproduce the scene of people rowing to save Qu Yuan.

Mid- Autumn Festival

The 15th day of the eighth lunar month is an important festival for the full moon during the autumn harvest. Family reunion to enjoy the moon, sharing mooncakes symbolizing reunion. Traditional flavors include lotus seed paste, five nuts, etc. In recent years, innovative mooncakes such as flowing heart and ice skin have been introduced.

the Pure Brightness Festival

On the 108th day after the Winter Solstice, it holds both seasonal and festive significance. People sweep tombs to worship their ancestors, repair tombs, offer flowers and fruits, and express their remembrance of their ancestors. At this time, the spring atmosphere is strong, and customs such as hiking, flying kites, and inserting willows are intertwined.
